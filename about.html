My Story

I was born and raised in Sugar Land, Texas, and am the proud daughter of immigrant parents who came to the United States to pursue PhDs in chemical engineering ‚Äî half Venezuelan, half Iranian. When I was little and people would ask what I wanted to be when I grew up, I would rattle off five different careers, convinced I could be multiple things at once or reinvent myself over and over. I didn't know what I wanted to study in college, but I chose accounting because working for myself one day sounded appealing, and a solid understanding of financials felt essential. I've stayed in consulting for so long because it has offered me variety, but I'm ready to own decisions, shape a company whose mission I truly believe in, and one day lead a P&L. This is why I'm pursuing Chief of Staff roles ‚Äî to learn directly from visionary leaders, drive execution at the highest level, and build the strategic muscle that comes from being in the room where decisions happen. I love challenges, embrace ambiguity, and consider myself a lifelong learner who thrives on solving tough problems. I'm drawn to research, writing, collaboration, and work that feels purposeful.
